'''Gilbert Wimple''' (fl. [[1994]])<ref name="age"/> was a  [[Wizardkind|wizard]] who worked for the [[Committee on Experimental Charms]] at the [[British Ministry of Magic|Ministry of Magic]].<ref name="GOF7"/>

==Biography==
Gilbert Wimple was born well before [[1994]],<ref name="age"/> and worked for the [[Committee on Experimental Charms]] at the [[British Ministry of Magic]]. Presumably as a result of working on the Committee, he [[Horn-growing spell|acquired horns]]. Wimple attended the [[1994 Quidditch World Cup]]. [[Arthur Weasley]] pointed him out to [[Harry Potter]], [[Ronald Weasley]], and [[Hermione Granger]].<ref name="GOF7">{{GOF|B|7}}</ref>

==Etymology==
The name "Gilbert" means "bright pledge", derived from the Germanic elements gisil "pledge, hostage" and beraht "bright". The Normans introduced this name to England, where it was common during the [[Middle Ages]]. It was borne by a 12th-century British saint, the founder of the religious order known as the Gilbertines.

{{w|Whimple}} is a village in Devon, near Exeter, where [[J. K. Rowling]] went to university. This could be a link to Wimple, as there are other references to places in the area in the books.
